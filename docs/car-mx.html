Climate Action Reserve Mexico Forest Protocol - Simplified Overview

Modified

July 10, 2024

Project Overview

Methodology Link

Forest Project: a planned set of defined activities designed to increase removals of CO2e from the atmosphere through increasing forest carbon stocks on the communal lands of ejidos or communities, private lands, or public lands.

Project Area: all areas within an ownership, held either communally, privately, or publicly, in which project activities within Activity Areas may occur as part of the project, currently or in the future.

  • Communally owned lands - PA must include all communcal ownership at project start.
  • dominio pleno (full ownership rights) parcels should be excluded from PA
  • ejidial (communal) parcels converted to dominio pleno should be removed and project re-verified
  • Define boundaries using a high resolution map that includes
    • major towns, roads, water
    • Geospatial file (.shp,.kml,.geojson)

Reporting Period: a period of time for which a Forest Owner quantifies and reports GHG removals (i.e., the length of time covered by the annual CMW) and submits monitoring reports for all required annual monitoring elements. A required duration of 12 months, with the exception of the first reporting period.

Activities

Mexico Forest Protocol will register activities for sustainable management of forests and enhancement of forest carbon stocks, adopted by the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC).

Note

Management actions may change over the course of the Activity Area’s life, the activity designation is based on the activity definition that is met at the Activity Area’s initiation.

Activities Definition Activity Area Criteria
Agroforestry and Silvopastoral Integration of trees into non-tree crop and animal farming systems May be developed within protected areas if activities are not prohibited by management plan. Agricultural product may be included.
Improved Forest Management (IFM) Enhances sequestration and resiliency of sequestered carbon Primary land cover is forest, present in varying densities and sizes, forest has management program.
Reforestation Direct planting of native tree seedlings or site preparation activities Occurs on landscapes that have not been forested for the past 5 years or have been impacted by natural disturbance.
Restoration Increases carbon stocks and canopy cover on degraded natural forests Eligible in any natural forest, must follow management program, not due to legal or regulatory impediment.

Eligibility

The protocol is applicable to forest projects anyhere in mexico so long as they meet the other eligibility criteria.

Documentation

The Reserve requires data transparency for all Forest Projects, including data that displays current carbon stocks, reversals, and verified GHG removals, as well as verification reports. For this reason, all non-confidential project data reported to the Reserve will be publicly available on the Reserve’s registry.

Forest Owner

Forest owners are the entity that has undisputed legal ownership of or jurisdiction over the forest carbon through outright ownership or through rights granted to them from a state or federal entity

The forest owner is responsible for undertaking a forest project and registering the project with CAR, and for all reporting. They can consult a project developer to assist with project implementation, but are ultuimately responsible for the project’s accuracy and completeness.

Note

Ownership can be private, communal (e.g. ejidos), or public.

Attestations

  1. Attestation of title must be formally provided to indicate that the owner has exclusive owenership claim to the GHG removals achived by their project over the verification period.

  2. Attestation of No Conflicts must be formally provided prior to each verification declaring that there are no disputes, claims, or lawsuits related to the property.

For all ejidos and communities, the Forest Owner must submit a document produced by the Agrarian Attorney (Procuraduría Agraria) that states that there are no agrarian conflicts within the Activity Areas as part of the Project Report.

  1. Attestation of Regulatory Compliance must be signed prior to each verification to indicate that the proejct is in complieance with laws, bylaws, regulations and norms relavant to the project activity.

Project Developer

A project developer may be the Forest Owner or an independent third-party contracted by the Forest Owner to assist the Forest Owner and implement the Forest Project. Non compliance must be delared in writing - Climate reserve tonnes (credits) will not be issued for GHG removals that occurred for the monitoring period in which the material non-compliance occurred

Aggregation

The goal of aggregation is to alleviate transaction costs for individual landowners and maintain standards and high-quality offsets. The aggregator may be a corporation or other legally constituted entity, city, county, state agency, individual or a combination thereof, which may then submit documentation on behalf of Aggregate participants (two or more Forest Owners).

Individual project activities can be combined with others to reduce administrative costs, improve marketing opportunities, and enable economies of scale.

Note

An Aggregate is two or more individual Forest Projects and may be composed of any combination of eligible Forest Owners from any geographic location within Mexico.

Aggregation only applies to:

  • Projects with < 10,000 ha of activity areas
  • Individual registration and signatory must be performed, including buffer pool assessments.
  • Reversal liability lies with individual forest owners

Investment into forest carbon projects has the potential to improve quality of life for rural communities, both in terms of increased revenues and in terms of sustaining and improving forest ecosystems.

Environmental Safeguards

Environmental safeguards ensure that Forest Projects sustain and/or enhance forest ecosystem functions. The environmental safeguards are established by activity to take into consideration the different forest ecosystems and land cover types present within different Activity Areas.

Environmental Safeguard (ES) Applicable Activities Activity Area Guidance
ES1 Maintenance of forest carbon stocks All Activity Areas must maintain or increase standing live and dead carbon stocks over the project life, as determined by a running 10-year average of carbon stocks within the Activity Areas. Exceptions may be granted by the Reserve.
ES2 Native Species IFM, Restoration, Reforestation Must demonstrate progress towards achieving 95% native species within the Activity Areas. Use of native species outside historic range allowed if used as climate change adaptation strategy with SEMARNAT office approval.
- IFM/Restoration: 50 years
- Reforestation: immediately after forest stand establishment.
ES3 Composition of Native Species IFM, Restoration, Reforestation Must demonstrate progress towards meeting native species composition according to Table 3.3. Exceptions allowed with regional SEMARNAT or CONABIO approval.
- IFM/Restoration: 50 years
- Reforestation: immediately after forest stand establishment.
ES4 Maintenance or increase of tree canopy cover IFM, Restoration, Reforestation, Agroforestry, Silvopastoral Tree canopy cover must not decrease due to human activities. Verify at 6th reporting period. Decline >5% due to negligence results in corrective action. Approved activities planned by government agencies are exempt.
ES5 Sustainable harvesting practices IFM Harvest areas >5 hectares must retain 10% of trees in groups no further than 100 meters from other trees. Retained trees not felled until 10 years old. Exceptions require prior written request approval.
ES6 Maintenance of natural land cover Reforestation Consider effects on ecological processes. Provide justification for activities impacting natural land cover for Reserve approval.
ES7 Soil disturbance during site preparation for tree planting All Site preparation using deep ripping prohibited from affecting >1% of Activity Area. If exceeded, reporting for the Activity Area is suspended for three periods.
Example: Deep ripping on 100-hectare area affects 22,000 meters with tine width of 0.1 meters, affecting 2.24% of area.
Note

Activty area size determines native species composition requirments:

  • 10ha - single species allowed
  • 10-50ha - 90% single species
  • 50-100ha - 80% single species, 2 other species required
  • 100-1000ha - 70% single species, 3 other species required
  • > 1000ha - 60% single species, 4 other species required

Forest Types

  1. Natural forest and shrub land cover (bosque, selva, zonas áridas) are where dominant overstory vegetation is comprised of an overwhelming majority of native species, i.e., greater than 70% by CO2e stocking.
  2. Natural grassland areas are where grassland plant communities (native or non-native species) exist due to natural environmental features (soil conditions, precipitation, etc.) and/or natural frequency of disturbance events.
Note

If the grasslands must be constantly grazed or burned more frequently than would be the case in a natural ecosystem, the grasslands would not meet the definition of natural.

  1. Wetlands are considered natural wherever they are found not being used for agriculture or intensive aquaculture and not part of a non-natural waterbody.

Start Date

The project start date cannot be more than 1 year prior to the submittal date and must be able to demonstrate project activities initiated on the claimed date through verifiable action or event. Permissable actions include:

  1. An Assembly where commencent of training or analysis for the project were approved - provided to the verifier with an official Assembly Act form, meeting notes, agenda.
  2. Project submission
Note

Addition of subsequent Activity Areas is based on official approval and inclusion of these areas in the annual MRV documents.

Crediting Period

Two crediting period lengths (durability commitments) are permitted:

  1. 30 Years
  2. 100 Years

Projects with a 30-year crediting pecriod that have met all Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) requirements and maintained legal compliance throughout their first crediting period, can extend the crediting period for another 30-year period using the baseline developed for the initial crediting period.

Minimum Time Commitment

Projects must commit to maintaining carbon sequestered due to project activities for a minimum period of 30 years up to the Reserve’s 100-year permanence requirement. However, for projects that commit to a period less than 100 years, credits will be issued in an amount proportionate to the length of the commitment relative to 100 years.

Additionality

Projects must yield GHG removals that are determined to be additonal to what would have opccured in the absense of a project.Projects must pass the following 2 tests to be considered additional:

  1. Legal - GHG removals must be greater than that required by any applicable laws/rules/statutes.
    • IFM - Demonstrate that actual forest growth exceeds allowable harvest from a management plan. Any non-harvested periodic growth is addtional.
    • Reforestation - Was not forest within past 5 years
    • Restoration - Risk to forest inventories are present at considerable levels
  2. Performance Standard - GHG removals must be greater than buisiness as usual activities

Direct/indirect investments are required for a project to be considered additional:

  1. Reforestation/Agroforestry
    • Direct - labour / capital (seedlings, planting, planning, site prep.)
    • Indirect - protecting established stands from herbivory, land conversion to grazing / agrilculture
  2. IFM / Urban forestry
    • Direct - stockign improvements, opportunty costs assocaited with extended rotations
    • Indirect - protection of stands from biotic/abiotic/manmade risks (e.g. illegal harvest)

Assessment Boundary

We provide included primary and secondary sources below. Excluded sources are not listed.

Intended changes in carbon stocks, GHG emissions or removals

Project: Measured by field measurements and updating forest carbon inventory through remeasurement and growth projections		 Increases in standing live carbon stocks are the primary effect of carbon enhancement projects.

Reforestation

Site Preparation

Activities to make conditions in an Activity Area more conducive to tree establishment will have secondary effect emissions associated with them as a result of the mobile combustion of fossil fuels for heavy equipment operation and the removal of shrub biomass. This is calculated where a standard emission factor (1.61), difference between shrub cover as a percent of activity area, and total activity area are multiplied.

Biomass Removal

Emissions associated with removal of shrub cover must be integrated. The same equation as the site preparation equation is used, with area replacing the standard emission factor.

Leakage

Reforestation, Restoration, Agroforestry, and Silvopastoral activities may result in a shift of grazing or agricultural activities outside the Project Area. This is assessed a single time with results used for the entirety of the project. IFM

Reduction in harvesting within the activity area could result in increases in harvestin outside. The amount of secondary effects is dependent on how much harvesting occurs on the Activity Area relative to the baseline scenario.

Net GHG Estimation

Following identification of primary and secondary GHG sources, the following 5 steps are used to conservatively estimate GHG removals.

  1. Quantify the Activity Area live and dead standing carbon - Each reporting period

  2. Determine the Activity Area baseline onsite carbon stocks - Initial standing carbon stock

Note

Projects are eligible to receive credits to the extent they increase forest carbon inventories above baseline levels within the Activity Areas.

  1. Calculating the Activity Area Primary Effect - Each reporting period
    • Quantify actual change in GHG removals by differencing carbon stocks from the previous reporting period, then
    • difference remaining carbon stocks with baseline for previous reporting period
  2. Quantify Activity Area Secondary Effects - Each reporting period
    • Conservative assessment of whether secondary sources of GHG emissions or removals were quantifiable
  3. Calculating total net GHG removals - Each reporting period
    • Sum primary and secondary effects
    • If positive GHG removals are present
    • If negative, there is a potential reversal

Activity Area Baseline

Baselines must be established independently for each activity area. After activity areas pass additionality criteria, the itial baseline is calculated as the sum of carbon (CO2e) in the required carbon pools at the start-date.

New activity areas can be added, with new baselines calculated for those areas.

Permanence

Note

All credited GHG’s must be effectively “permanent” (stored for at least 100 years). Tonne-year accounting principles, as described in Appendix F are used to quantify the time-value of storing carbon as a relative proportion of the 100-year permanence requirement.

Credits are issued based on the proportion of carbon that is stored or secured for the period of time the carbon is committed (e.g. 30 years) relative to the 100-year permanence period.

Projects that commit to maintaining carbon sequestered for a period of time less than 100 years, credits are issued based on the proportion of carbon that is stored or secured for the period of time the carbon is committed relative to the 100-year permanence period.

Mechanisms are used to meet GHG permanence obligations:

  1. Legal contracts (e.g. Project Implementation Agreement) / formal commitments (communal/ejidal projects).
    • Commitment to maintain carbon sequestered due to project activities
    • Comply with MRV requirements
  2. Buffer Pool insurance based on the projects risk profile
  3. Economic incentives including buffer pool dividends for projects with ongoing compliance and maintenance of carbon stocks

Reversals

If a reversal occurs, credits must be retired to ensure permanence of the CRTs already issued. These are detected during annul monitorign and verification (forest owners / third party verifiers).

  1. The release of removal GHG prior to fulfilling its permanence commitment.
  2. If quantified GHG removals are negative for a given period
Note

Avoidable Reversals - the result of negligent human activities.

  • Forest Owner must retire credits based on below formula within 2 years, else contract restitution clause is triggered

Unavoidable Reversals - the result of natural events (fire, insect mortality, windthrow)

  • If Reserve agrees the reversal was unavoidable, credits will be retired from the buffer pool.
  • Buffer pool dividends will not be paid until buffer pool credits are restored

Computational Reversals - reversal as a result of protocol calculations (e.g. secondary effects)

  • Not based on on-the-ground activities, but indicates over-crediting
  • Forest owner will “true-up” at next verification by deducting reversed quantity from newly issued credits
    • No growth in that year will result in cancellation of credits from previous vintages

Reversal Example

Most recently issued credits are reversed first, proceeding backwards in time.

A project was credited 100 tonnes of carbon in year 1 and another 50 tonnes in year 2. In year 3, a reversal occurs that releases 75 credits to the atmosphere. The 50 credits issued in year 2 are considered reversed, along with 25 of the credits issued in year 1.

Buffer Pool

A general insurance mechanism against reversals. A holding account for credits that is administered by the Reserve. Determined through a project-specific risk assessment.

All projects must contribute a percentage of credits to the buffer pool any time they are issued. Credits are contributed for unavoidable and avoidable reversals. Projects are incentivized with a future stream of credits (dividends) based on compliance.

Important

If a reversal lowers the Forest Project’s actual standing live or dead carbon stocks below its approved baseline standing live or dead carbon stocks, the Forest Project will automatically be terminated.

If the Forest Project is terminated due to an Unavoidable Reversal, another project may be initiated and submitted to the Reserve for registration on the same Project Area. New projects may not be initiated on the same Project Area if the Forest Project is terminated due to an Avoidable Reversal.

Risk Types

  1. Financial Risk - Financial failure of an organization resulting in bankruptcy
  2. Management Risk - risk of management activities that directly or indirectly could lead to a reversal.
    • Illegal Removals of Forest Biomass
    • Conversion of Project Area to Alternative Land Uses
    • Over-Harvesting
  3. Social and Political Risk - changes in social governance and communal or ejidal acceptance of the project.
Important

The risks of social actions leading to reversals are significant for ejidos and communities but can be mitigated through actions to ensure ongoing participation and oversight of project benefits.

Social risk are considered to be minimal or private and public lands.

  1. Natural Disturbance Risk
    • Wildfire, Disease, or Insect Outbreak
    • Other Episodic Catastrophic Events (e.g. Hurricane)

Verification

Standardized approaches for verification are presented in the Reserves Verification Program Manual.

Important

Only Reserve-approved Mexico Forest Project verification bodies are eligible to verify Mexico Forest Project reports.

While Forest Owners may depend on consultants or project developers to complete project requirements, responsibility for monitoring reports and verification compliance is assigned to the Forest Owner.

Initial Verification

All Forest Projects must be initially verified within 36 months of their start date.

Must confirm the project’s: 1. Eligibility 2. Compliance with applicable social and environmental safeguards 3. That included Activity Areas’ initial inventory and baseline have been established in conformance with the MFP.

The verification body must assess and ensure the completeness and accuracy of all required reporting elements for the Project Report (see Section 7.1.2), presented in Table 7.1.

Details

IFM

Site visit is mandatory.

Reforestation Activities

Site visit not mandatory. Can be verified through use of geotagged photos, documented evidence of the purchase or receipt of trees, or through an onsite confirmation.

Full Verification

Review of the Forest Project’s eligibility, quantification, and adherence to environmental and social safeguards (if applicable). Requirements vary by activity (i.e., IFM, Reforestation, etc.)

  1. Forest Projects not in an Aggregate must undergo a full verification every six reporting periods or in the event of adding a new Activity Area.
Details

IFM

Site visit is mandatory for IFM. Forest Owner and local communituy / ejido can support verification, but verification body should independently take requirement measurements.

Reforestation Activities

Site visit is mandatory.

Desk Verification

Complete verifications for reporting periods in between required full verifications. the verification body must review the data in the annual monitoring reports to check calculations and information for reasonability, accuracy, and completeness.

Answers the question Is the information presented in annual monitoring reports reasonable? by independently comparing:

  1. carbon stock changes and increment
  2. inventory data and projected growth
Note

Desk verifications do not include a field inspection of reported data unless the verifier determines a field visit is required to resolve a specific conflict. Any fluctuations must be fully justified.

Site Visits

Site visits are conducted to verify forest carbon stock and are mandatory for full verifications.

  1. Sequential sampling
    • Sample plots to meet a minimum number of successive plots that indicate agreement.
    • Randomly output plots for sequential sampling
    • Depends on the number of Activity Areas using field sampling inventories comprising the project and/or whether the inventory is stratified
    • Height a diameter sequential sampling - if they match data move on to other plot components
  2. Minimum precision - +/- 20% at the 90% confidence interval
  3. Measurements - Fixed area plots
    • DBH
    • Heights
    • Species
    • Defect / Decay

Completing Verification

  1. Verification Report delivered to the Forest Owner.
  2. Detailed List of Findings.
  3. Verification Statement detailing vintage and number of GHG removals.
  4. Verify that the number of GHG removals, as well as the reversal risk rating, specified in the Verification Report and Statement match the number entered into the Reserve system.
  5. Conduct exit meeting with Forest Owner
  6. With reasonable level of assurance, upload electronic copies of (into the Reserve system):
    • Verification Report
    • List of Findings
    • Verification Statement
    • Optional Verification Activity Log .
  7. Return important records and documents to the Forest Owner.
